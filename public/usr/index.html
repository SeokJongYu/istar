USR & USRCAT

ligand-based virtual screening powered by ultrafast shape recognition techniques

a new version is available at http://usr.marseille.inserm.fr, screening 94 million conformers and running 500 times faster

Submitted jobs

Submit a new job

The input to USR is onefold:

  • A query molecule in 3D format. Totally 23,129,083 molecules collected from the All Clean subset of ZINC are to be screened.

The output from USR is twofold:

  • hits.csv.gz: ZINC ID, USR score and USRCAT score of the top 10000 hit molecules geometrically and pharmacophorically similar to the query molecule.
  • hits.pdbqt.gz: structures of the top 1000 hit molecules.

Tutorials

How to submit a new job

Open your query molecule locally in the WebGL canvas. The supported file formats are:

  • MOL2. Your mol2 file must contain @<TRIPOS>ATOM and @<TRIPOS>BOND records. Here is a sample file.
  • SDF. Your sdf file must contain atoms and bonds. Here is a sample file.
  • XYZ. Your xyz file must contain atoms. Bonds will be recovered automatically. Here is a sample file.
  • PDB. Your pdb file must contain HETATM and CONECT records. Here is a sample file.
  • PDBQT. Your pdbqt file must contain ATOM records. Bonds will be recovered automatically. Here is a sample file.

The provided molecule file must contain exactly one query molecule.

The WebGL canvas supports the following keyings:

  • Default: rotate
  • Ctrl: translate
  • Shift: zoom in/out
  • Ctrl + shift: slab

Briefly describe your job. The job description is defaulted to your query molecule filename.

Fill in your email to receive notification upon job completion. Fill in any anonymous email should you not want to receive notification.

Submit your job.

Check your job status in the topmost table.

Screening libraries

23,129,083 molecules were collected from versions 2012-04-26, 2013-01-10 and 2013-12-18 of the All Clean subset of ZINC and converted into PDBQT format. We thank Professor John J. Irwin, the developer and maintainer of ZINC, for granting us permission to use ZINC with three conditions:

  • We shall provide links to http://zinc.docking.org/substance/zincid for top hits so that users can seek for the most current purchasing information at ZINC's official web site.
  • We shall limit the number of hits for download to 1000 molecules from a single job.
  • We shall update our data when ZINC data is updated so that users can benefit from the most current information.

3,152 approved drugs were collected from dbap, fda and npc catalogs of ZINC. Duplicate compounds were removed.
