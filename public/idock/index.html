New job

The input to idock is threefold, a receptor in PDBQT format, a search space defined by center and size, and a set of ligands to dock. The receptor should be generated by the prepare_receptor4.py Python script of MGLTools with polar hydrogens present, and should start with ATOM records and end with a TER record. The search space should define a binding site of the receptor in cuboid shape and should be in unit â„«. Totally 12,171,187 ligands are collected from the All Clean subset version 2012-04-06 of ZINC and converted into PDBQT format. Filtering conditions on 9 molecular properties (e.g. mwt, logp, hbd, hba) can be specified as closed intervals. Only the ligands satisfying all the 9 filtering conditions will be docked. Upon receival of your job, our workstations execute a customized version of idock 1.5 and perform two-phase docking. In phase 1, ligands will be docked in a coarse but fast manner, and will be sorted in the ascending order of predicted free energy. In phase 2, the top 1000 ligands will be docked again in a fine but slow manner. As result, the sorted list of predicted free energy produced in phase 1 and the predicted conformations and purchasing information of the top 1000 ligands produced in phase 2 will be provided for download, according to our agreement with Prof. John J. Irwin, the major developer and maintainer of ZINC. Currently there are four workstations running idock 1.5 in the background.

Number of ligands satisfying all the 9 filtering conditions: 188,820
