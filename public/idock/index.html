idock

a multithreaded virtual screening tool for flexible ligand docking

New job

The input to idock is threefold:

  • A receptor in PDBQT format. The receptor should be generated by the prepare_receptor4.py Python script of MGLTools with polar hydrogens present. The receptor can contain one or more chains, which should start with ATOM records and end with a TER record.
  • A search space defined by a center and a size in unit â„«. The search space should define a binding site (e.g. enzymatic site or allosteric site) of the receptor in cuboid shape.
  • A set of ligands to dock. Totally 12,171,187 ligands are collected from ZINC. Filtering conditions on 9 molecular properties (e.g. mwt, logp, hbd, hba, nrb) can be specified as closed intervals. Only the ligands satisfying all the 9 filtering conditions will be docked.

Our workstations execute idock 1.5 and perform two-phase docking:

  • In phase 1, the selected ligands will be docked in a coarse but fast manner.
  • In phase 2, the top 1000 ligands will be docked again in a fine but slow manner.

As result, you can download:

  • phase1.csv.gz: the predicted free energy and 9 molecular properties of the selected ligands produced in phase 1
  • phase2.csv.gz: the predicted free energy and 9 molecular properties as well as link to purchasing information of the top 1000 ligands produced in phase 2
  • hits.pdbqt.gz: the predicted conformations of the top 1000 ligands produced in phase 2

Number of ligands satisfying all the 9 filtering conditions: 188,820

Ligands

The ligands are collected from version 2012-04-06 of the All Clean subset of ZINC and converted into PDBQT format. We are granted by Prof. John J. Irwin, the developer and maintainer of ZINC, permission to use ZINC with three conditions:

  • We shall provide links to http://zinc.docking.org/substance/zincid for top hits so that users can seek for the most current purchasing information at ZINC's official web site.
  • We shall limit the number of top hits for download to 1000 ligands from a single job.
  • We shall update our ligands when ZINC data is updated so that users can benefit from the most current ligand data.
